Exam Overview｜题型与复习策略

I. Multiple Choice

30%｜15 questions，每题2分

Choose the answer that best completes each statement. 重点：术语定义、作品细节、韵式/格律、叙述视角、人物类型、戏剧类型。

II. True / False

20%｜20 questions，每题1分

Choose A if the statement is true, and B if it is false. 重点：绝对化表述、概念偷换、作品形式与主题、术语定义反向判断。

III. Short-Answer Questions

10%｜2 questions｜辨析题

Provide a concise answer that identifies and explains the key points. 重点：概念对比、定义差异、核心判断维度。

IV. Brief Analysis Questions

10%｜2 questions｜简析题

Give a short answer about the literary work. 重点：结合具体作品分析 imagery, diction, tone, characterization, symbol, POV 等。

V. Analytical Essay

30%｜1 text｜赏析题

Based on the given excerpt, write a short comprehensive essay according to the specified requirements. 必须形成连贯分析，不能只列要点。

最高优先级：先按题型分配时间：客观题保定义与作品识别；辨析题背“定义 + 比较维度 + 核心差异”；简析题与赏析题训练“证据 → 手法 → 效果 → 主题”的完整表达。

层级作品材料依据备考动作

1. Rhyme 与 Sound

Rhyme：the formalized consonance of syllables；复习时先判断 stressed vowel 是否一致，再看 consonant 是否一致。

类别术语英文解释课件例子 / 识别点
Vowel rhymes
元音韵		Full / perfect / exact rhymestressed vowel and all following sounds matchfish/dish; why/sigh; power/flower。不是只看拼写。
Assonanceonly the stressed vowel is the same / vowel sound repetitionshake/hate; time/mind；和 consonance 相反。
Consonant rhymes
辅音韵		Alliteration / head rhymeonly the beginning consonant is the samesafe and sound; fish or fowl；关键词 initial / beginning。
Consonanceending consonant or consonant sounds matchfish/dash; cold/bald；不是 vowel sounds。
Para-rhymeall consonants match while stressed vowel differsblock/black; reader/rider；属于 consonant rhyme。
Visual rhyme
视觉韵		Eye rhymesimilar spelling but different pronunciation; appeals to the eyeblood/hood; daughter/laughter。
Types
押韵类型		Masculine rhymeends on a stressed syllablesmile/file; design/resign；不是“男性主题”。
Feminine rhymeends with an unstressed syllablesmiling/filing; picky/tricky；通常多音节。
Position
押韵位置		End rhymerhyming words appear at the end of lines看诗行末尾。
Internal rhymerhyming words appear in the middle of a line or around a caesuraI met a man with a skin of tan；看行内或停顿前后。

2. Stanza / Rhyme Scheme / Line Movement

Rhyme scheme：stanza + rhyme，即“行数 + 押韵方式”。

Stanza names

  • 2 lines: couplet
  • 3 lines: tercet / triplet
  • 4 lines: quatrain
  • 5 lines: quintain / cinquain
  • 6 lines: sextain
  • 7 lines: septet
  • 8 lines: octave
  • 9 lines: Spenserian stanza
  • 14 lines: sonnet

High-frequency rhyme schemes

  • Heroic couplet 英雄偶句诗: aa bb cc...
  • Ballad quatrain 民谣体四行诗: abcb / abab
  • Terza rima 三行体: aba bcb cdc
  • Spenserian stanza 斯宾塞式诗节: ababbcbcc

Sonnets 十四行诗

  • Shakespearean / English sonnet: abab cdcd efef gg
  • Petrarchan / Italian sonnet: octave + sestet
  • Petrarchan example from slides: abbaabba cdccdc
  • Spenserian sonnet: abab bcbc cdcd ee

Enjambment / Run-on line 跨行

  • A sentence or poetic argument runs beyond the line ending without a full pause or punctuation.
  • 一诗行不一定是完整句子；意思延续到下一行。
  • 读跨行句时，行末停顿较短。

End-stopped line 结句行

  • A line whose syntactic unit is completed at the line end.
  • 一行诗正好构成相对完整的句子或意思。
  • 通常有较明显停顿，和 run-on line 相对。

3. Meter 与 Rhythm

Syllable 音节→Foot 音步→Meter 格律→Rhythm 节奏

Meter is described by both the type and the number of the predominating foot；所以答题时通常写成 “iambic/trochaic + pentameter/tetrameter”。

术语中文英文定义 / 结构识别点
Syllable音节the sound unit that forms the basis of foot先看重读/非重读，再组合成 foot。
Foot音步the basic unit in the description of the underlying rhythm of a poem由音节的重读/非重读类型与数量界定。
Iamb / Iambus抑扬格unstressed + stressed (× /)today, compare；常与 pentameter 组合。
Trochee扬抑格stressed + unstressed (/ ×)English, college；可组成 trochaic tetrameter。
Dactyl扬抑抑格stressed + two unstressed (/ × ×)actually, negative, poetry。
Anapaest抑抑扬格two unstressed + stressed (× × /)interrupt, understand。
Spondee扬扬格two stressed syllables (/ /)常作格律变体或强调，不作为最常见基础格。
Pyrrhic抑抑格two unstressed syllables (× ×)常与其他音步一起分析，单独作为主格较少。
Meter格律the recurring pattern of stress and pause in a line由 predominating foot 的类型和数量共同命名。
Iambic pentameter抑扬格五音步five predominantly iambic feet per line: × / × / × / × / × /Shakespearean sonnet 高频；blank verse 也用它。
Trochaic tetrameter扬抑格四音步four predominantly trochaic feet per line: / × / × / × / ×The Tyger 练习例；“Soliloquy of the Spanish Cloister” 大体为 rough trochaic tetrameter。
Rhythm节奏the felt movement created by stress, pause, meter, sound and syntax比 meter 更宽泛；tone 也会受 rhythm 影响。

Number of feet｜每行音步数量

名称音步数考试识别点
monometerone foot单音步。
dimetertwo feet双音步。
trimeterthree feet三音步。
tetrameterfour feet如 trochaic tetrameter。
pentameterfive feet如 iambic pentameter；sonnet 高频。

heptameter / octameterseven / eight feet七音步 / 八音步。

Metric variation 格律变异

  • Definition: deviation from the expected metrical pattern.
  • Effect: emphasis, or avoiding mechanical monotony.
  • Types: inversion, headless verse, catalexis.

Blank verse 素体诗

  • Has meter but no rhyme.
  • Unrhymed iambic pentameter.
  • Also called heroic verse.
  • Examples: Shakespeare’s plays; Milton’s Paradise Lost.

Free verse 自由诗

  • Without fixed metrical patterns.
  • May be rhymed or unrhymed.
  • Written in lines, but not controlled by regular meter.
  • Example: Walt Whitman’s Leaves of Grass.

4. Imagery 与 Diction

Imagery 意象

  • Representation through language of sense experience: sight, sound, smell, taste, touch, internal sensation, motion.
  • Communicates not information, but experience.
  • Not to tell, but to show.
  • Purpose: to evoke vivid experience and convey feelings and ideas.

Diction 措辞

  • Poetry is the art of language.
  • 关注 sound, denotation, connotation。
  • 诗歌语言倾向 short and concise, concrete, aesthetic, suggestive, multidimensional。
  • 追求 novelty / defamiliarization，而不是直接传递信息。

5. Voice: Speaker and Tone

Speaker 说话者

  • Poet / author ≠ speaker.
  • Reliable speaker: attitude largely represents the poet’s.
  • Unreliable speaker: attitude differs from the poet’s, often in dramatic monologues.

Tone 语气

  • Emotional coloring or meaning.
  • Makes the attitude of the poem clear to readers.
  • Examples: affectionate, solemn, gloomy, nostalgic, sarcastic, ironic.
  • Meter, rhyme, word choice, syntax, figures and imagery all help produce tone.

6. Classification of Poetry

类型课件定义代表作/识别点
Epiclengthy narrative poem in elevated, ceremonious style about heroic deeds significant to a culture or nationIliad, Odyssey, Beowulf；hero，不是 knight。
Balladfolk song or orally transmitted poem telling a popular story in a simple dramatic wayRobin Hood ballads；常见四行诗节。
Romancemedieval narrative poem about knightly adventureSir Gawain and the Green Knight；此处不是“浪漫爱情”。
Odelofty lyric addressing/praising a person, thing, event or natureOde to the West Wind。
Elegylyric lamenting a dead friend or public figure核心是 lament，不只是 praise。
Dramatic monologuea single persona speaks to an implied auditor and is overheard by the reader无作者评论；speaker 在辩解中不自觉暴露性格。

1. Rise and development

  • 18th century：一般被视为 English novel 的诞生期。
  • 条件：Enlightenment 对 reason / actuality 的重视；印刷技术与市场发展；城市人口识字率提高。

4. Characterization

简答题重点：Two ways of characterization = direct characterization + indirect characterization。
  • Direct characterization：the narrator/author directly tells or comments on a character’s appearance or personality；常以外貌、名称、直接说明呈现。
  • Indirect characterization：the author steps aside and lets characters reveal themselves through dialogue, action, behavior, feeling and others’ reactions；reader infers the trait。

1. Aristotle’s Six Elements

Plot→Character→Thought→Diction→Spectacle→Music

Plot

“the soul of tragedy”；情节结构、冲突与因果。

Thought

the reasoning aspect；概括 moral 并指向全剧 symbolic meaning。

Diction

dynamic、clear、oral、interesting but economical、appropriate to character and situation。

Spectacle / Music

Spectacle 指 scenery, lighting, costume, make-up, movement 等视觉方面；Music 指 sound / silence 与 spoken word 的 tonal pattern，用来加强 mood。

2. Plot Structure

Exposition→Point of attack / conflict→Rising action & complication→Climax / reversal→Falling action→Denouement
TermFunction判断点
Expositionsets action in time and place, introduces characters and initiates conflict必须 clear and quick；也可放入 foreshadowing。
Foreshadowingearly clues make later action credible, build suspense and tension也能揭示人物、推动 complication。
Complicationdevelops the conflict with depth and breadth冲突复杂化，不只是“出现问题”。
Climax / reversalturning point or highest tension之后进入 falling action。
Denouement / resolutionthe ending that resolves or settles the action结局、收束。

1. Three Waves

First wave (1848–1920)

abolitionism and women’s suffrage；1920–1960 有 setback。

Second wave (mid-1960s–1982)

pill、abortion、equal pay；揭示 sexual harassment、domestic violence。

3. Premises & Practices

  • Women are oppressed economically, politically, socially and psychologically by patriarchy and patriarchal ideology.
  • Western institutions and literary canon are deeply rooted in male-oriented thinking.
  • Biology determines sex；culture determines gender；gender concerns socially programmed behavior, not anatomy.
  • Ultimate goal：promote women’s equality and change the world.
  • Practices：gender studies；sex-related writing strategies；Marxist feminism；psychoanalytic feminism；minority feminist criticism。

Trifles application: patriarchy appears in men’s dismissal of women’s domestic knowledge; sisterhood emerges when Mrs. Hale and Mrs. Peters protect Minnie. See Drama key slides 10–12.

Brief Analysis Questions｜简析题模板

1. General brief analysis structure（简析通用结构）

The passage/detail uses ______ to present ______. More specifically, the word/image/action “______” suggests ______. Therefore, this detail contributes to the theme of ______.

2. Characterization

There are two major ways of characterization: direct and indirect characterization. Direct characterization tells the reader what a character is like through explicit description or comment. Indirect characterization allows the character’s personality to emerge through speech, action, behavior, feeling, and other characters’ reactions.

Story vs Plot 因果 / 时间

Story：a narrative of events arranged in their time sequence.

Plot：a narrative of events with emphasis on causality；time sequence remains, but causality overshadows it.

Story: “The king died and then the queen died.”
Plot: “The king died and then the queen died of grief.”

Round vs Flat Character

Round：well-developed, complex, three-dimensional, rich and convincing；may contain contradictory qualities.

Flat：underdeveloped, one-dimensional or stereotypical；often describable by one or two traits.

核心维度：complexity / degree of development。

Static vs Dynamic Character

Static：does not undergo significant change.

Dynamic：undergoes significant change or development.

易错：round/flat 主要看复杂度，static/dynamic 主要看是否变化。材料中常把 round 与 dynamic、flat 与 static 配对描述，但答辨析题时应分清两个判断维度。
Direct vs Indirect Characterization 连续真题

Direct：the narrator/author directly states or comments on appearance and personality — telling.

Indirect：the author lets speech, action, behavior, feeling and others’ reactions reveal personality — showing and inference.

Protagonist / Antagonist / Foil

Protagonist：main character involved in the conflict driving the plot；不必然是 moral hero。

Antagonist：a character or force in conflict with the protagonist；可为 nature / society / fate。

Foil：through difference or similarity, brings out an aspect of another character。

Blank Verse vs Free Verse

Blank verse：unrhymed iambic pentameter — meter without rhyme.

Free verse：without regular metrical patterns；may be rhymed or unrhymed.

End-stopped Line vs Run-on Line / Enjambment 诗歌行末停顿

End-stopped line：a poetic line whose syntactic unit or sense unit is completed at the line ending；行末意思相对完整，通常有明显停顿。

Run-on line / Enjambment：a line in which the sentence or poetic argument runs beyond the line ending without a full pause or punctuation；意思延续到下一行，行末停顿较短。

核心维度：whether the sentence / meaning stops at the line ending.

An end-stopped line gives a sense of closure and regularity, whereas a run-on line creates continuity, movement and sometimes suspense.

Alliteration / Assonance / Consonance

Alliteration：initial consonant repetition；Assonance：vowel sound repetition；Consonance：consonant sound repetition, especially non-initial/end patterns in the course materials.

Tragedy vs Comedy

Tragedy：concentrates on man’s destiny and philosophical man；通过 pity/fear 与 katharsis 显示人面对毁灭性力量时的尊严。

Comedy：lives in commonplace everyday life and focuses on social man；揭示 follies, foibles and vices，通常以肯定性和快乐结局收束。

Exam-ready Memorization Paragraph｜考试背诵段落

William Blake’s “London” is a powerful criticism of the social and political oppression of eighteenth-century London. Through vivid imagery, dark diction, and repeated sounds, Blake presents the city as a place filled with misery, control, and institutional corruption.

First, the poem creates a gloomy and oppressive atmosphere through its diction. Words such as “charter’d,” “weakness,” “woe,” “cry,” “ban,” and “manacles” suggest restriction and suffering. The repetition of “every” emphasizes that pain is everywhere in the city and that no one can escape from it.

Second, Blake uses striking images to reveal the social reality of London. The “marks of weakness, marks of woe” show visible human suffering, while “mind-forg’d manacles” suggests psychological oppression. The “black’ning Church” and “Palace walls” symbolize the corruption of religious and political institutions. The oxymoron “Marriage hearse” connects marriage with death, showing that even love and family life are destroyed by social disease.

Formally, the poem consists of four quatrains with a regular ABAB rhyme scheme. This steady rhythm is like the speaker’s walk through the city, but the dark images make the walk increasingly disturbing. Therefore, “London” is not merely a description of a city, but a severe critique of oppression, suffering, and the loss of innocence.

Exam-ready Memorization Paragraph｜考试背诵段落

Shakespeare’s “Sonnet 130” is a realistic and ironic love poem. It challenges the conventional tradition of love poetry, in which women are often praised through exaggerated and artificial comparisons. Instead of idealizing his mistress, the speaker describes her in ordinary and realistic terms.

The poem uses negation and irony as its main techniques. The speaker says that his mistress’ eyes are “nothing like the sun,” her lips are less red than coral, and her cheeks are not like roses. These comparisons seem unflattering at first, but they actually reject false and exaggerated praise. By refusing to compare his mistress to perfect natural images, the speaker makes his love more honest and believable.

The diction of the poem is plain and direct. This simple language matches the poem’s theme of sincerity. The final couplet changes the tone of the whole poem. The speaker makes clear that his love is still rare and valuable, not because his mistress is perfect, but because his love is based on truth.

Therefore, “Sonnet 130” is not an anti-love poem. It is a poem about genuine love. Shakespeare suggests that real love does not need false beauty or artificial comparison; it values the beloved as a real and imperfect human being.

Emily Dickinson’s “I like to See it Lap the Miles” is a vivid poem about a train. Although the train is never directly named, the poem clearly presents it as a powerful moving creature. Through personification, imagery, and dynamic diction, Dickinson explores industrialization, power, and human ingenuity.

The most important device in the poem is personification. The train is described as if it were a living animal: it “lap[s] the Miles,” “lick[s] the Valleys up,” and “feed[s] itself at Tanks.” These verbs make the machine seem energetic, hungry, and alive. The train is no longer only a mechanical object; it becomes a powerful creature moving through the landscape.

The imagery also emphasizes the train’s size and strength. It can take a “prodigious step” around mountains and look into small houses by the roads. The sound of the train is described as “horrid” and “hooting,” and it “neigh[s] like Boanerges,” which compares it to a loud and powerful horse-like being. These images show both the wonder and the frightening power of modern technology.

At the end, the train becomes “docile and omnipotent” at its “stable door.” This contrast suggests that the train is both obedient and powerful. Therefore, the poem presents industrial progress with admiration, but also with an awareness of its overwhelming force.

Robert Browning’s “Soliloquy of the Spanish Cloister” is a dramatic and ironic poem that reveals the hypocrisy of a jealous monk. Through the speaker’s angry inner monologue, Browning explores envy, moral corruption, and religious hypocrisy.

The poem is powerful because the speaker unintentionally exposes himself. He condemns Brother Lawrence and presents himself as morally superior, but his violent language and sinful thoughts reveal the opposite. His curses, hatred, and jealousy show that he is far from the Christian virtues he claims to defend. Browning also uses colloquial diction and dramatic voice. Expressions such as “Gr-r-r” and angry curses make the poem sound like spontaneous speech. This creates immediacy and allows readers to enter the speaker’s private thoughts. The irony is clear: the speaker is a monk, but his mind is full of hatred, revenge, and moral ugliness.

Formally, the poem consists of nine eight-line stanzas with a regular rhyme scheme. The regular form contrasts with the speaker’s chaotic emotions. Therefore, the poem is not simply about personal hatred; it is a dramatic exposure of hypocrisy and corruption hidden behind outward piety.

Susan Glaspell’s Trifles is a one-act play that explores gender roles, social expectations, and justice. Set in a bleak rural kitchen, the play revolves around the investigation of John Wright’s murder. Through the contrast between male and female perspectives, Glaspell criticizes the marginalization of women’s experiences.

The men in the play view the house only as a crime scene. They search for official evidence and dismiss domestic details as “trifles.” However, the women understand the kitchen as a home and read the small details carefully. The broken jars, the unfinished quilt, the dead bird, and the broken cage all reveal Minnie Wright’s lonely and oppressed life. Ironically, the “trifles” ignored by men become the real clues to the truth.

Symbolism is central to the play. The cold kitchen suggests a loveless marriage. The dead bird symbolizes Minnie’s lost happiness and silenced voice. The cage represents marriage as confinement. The knot refers not only to sewing, but also to the rope around John Wright’s neck and the bond of female solidarity.

Therefore, Trifles is more than a murder mystery. It is a feminist critique of patriarchy and legal justice. Glaspell suggests that women’s knowledge, empathy, and shared experience can reveal truths that male authority fails to see.

Sherwood Anderson’s The Egg is a short story about failure, ambition, and the disillusionment of the American Dream. Shakespeare’s A Midsummer Night’s Dream is a romantic comedy that explores love, imagination, illusion, and transformation. The play presents love not as a purely rational choice, but as something unstable, changeable, and often irrational.

One important feature of the play is its complicated plot structure. Several groups of characters are involved: the Athenian lovers, the fairies, and the craftsmen. These different plot lines are connected by the forest, a dream-like space where ordinary rules are suspended. In the forest, love becomes confused and identities become unstable.
