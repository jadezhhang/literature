Exam Overview｜题型与复习策略

I. Multiple Choice

30%｜15 questions｜每题2分

Choose the answer that best completes each statement. 主要考术语定义、作品识别、流派/代表作、押韵与格律、叙述视角、人物类型、戏剧类型。

II. True / False

20%｜20 questions｜每题1分

Choose A if true and B if false. 注意绝对化表述、概念偷换、形式与主题的反向判断。

III. Short-Answer

10%｜2 questions｜辨析题

Provide a concise answer that identifies and explains the key points. 答法：定义 + 比较维度 + 核心差异 + 文学分析用途。

IV. Brief Analysis

10%｜2 questions｜简析题

Give a short answer about a literary work. 答法：具体细节/引文 + 手法 + 效果 + 主题。

V. Analytical Essay

30%｜1 text｜赏析题

Based on the given excerpt, write a short comprehensive essay. 要写成连贯短文，不能只列 bullet points。

最高优先级：辨析题先背 sex/gender, direct/indirect characterization, round/flat character, story/plot, modernism/modernity/modernization；客观题保 poetry prosody 与 drama genres；赏析题练六个核心文本。

复习顺序

顺序先做什么原因
1先背辨析题老师重点提纲说明第三题主要考对比题，且往年反复出现 sex/gender 与 characterization。
2再过客观题术语poetry prosody、fiction terms、drama genres 容易出选择/判断。

1. Classification of Poetry｜诗歌分类

核心：Poetry can be roughly classified into narrative poetry, lyric poetry, and dramatic poetry. 客观题一般考“某种诗属于哪一类”，赏析题则用它判断说话方式和文本功能。

Narrative poetry｜叙事诗

  • Epic: long narrative poem about cultural heroes and grand adventures; e.g. Beowulf.
  • Ballad: folk narrative poem, often in quatrains with simple colloquial language; e.g. Robin Hood ballads.
  • Romance: medieval fiction in verse or prose about chivalry, adventure and love; e.g. Sir Gawain and the Green Knight.

Lyric poetry｜抒情诗

  • Expresses personal feeling, meditation, or a concentrated moment of emotion.
  • Common forms: epigram, elegy, ode, aubade, sestina, sonnet, villanelle.
  • Exam clue: usually focuses on speaker, tone, imagery, diction and feeling rather than plot.

Dramatic poetry｜戏剧诗

  • Presented through a speaker, persona, or dramatic situation.
  • Dramatic monologue: an implied audience, no real dialogue; speaker reveals the self through speech.
  • Dramatic soliloquy: a character speaks aloud, often revealing inner thought.

2. Stanza / Rhyme Scheme / Line Movement

Stanza 看行数；rhyme scheme 看每行末尾的押韵模式；line movement 看句意是否在行末完成。
行数 / 形式名称识别点
2 linesCouplet / distich双行诗；heroic couplet 常见 aa bb cc。
3 linesTercet / triplet三行诗；terza rima 用 aba bcb cdc。
4 linesQuatrain四行诗节；ballad 常用 abcb 或 abab。
5/6/7/8 linesQuintain / sextain / septet / octave记名称即可；octave 常与 Petrarchan sonnet 相关。
9 linesSpenserian stanzaababbcbcc。
14 linesSonnet通常与 iambic pentameter、固定 rhyme scheme 一起考。

High-frequency rhyme schemes

  • Heroic couplet: aa bb cc...
  • Ballad: abcb / abab.
  • Terza rima: aba bcb cdc...
  • Ottava rima: abababcc.
  • Spenserian stanza: ababbcbcc.

Sonnet forms

  • Shakespearean / English sonnet: abab cdcd efef gg.
  • Petrarchan / Italian sonnet: octave + sestet; often abbaabba cde/cdc patterns.
  • Spenserian sonnet: abab bcbc cdcd ee.

End-stopped line

A line whose syntactic unit or sense unit is completed at the line ending. 行末意思较完整，停顿较明显。

Run-on line / Enjambment

The sentence or poetic argument runs beyond the line ending without a full pause or punctuation. 意思延续到下一行，行末停顿较短。

3. Rhyme 与 Sound

Rhyme: the formalized consonance of syllables. 判断时先看 stressed vowel，再看 following sounds / consonants。

维度术语核心定义例子 / 易错点
Vowel rhymesFull / perfect / exact rhymestressed vowel and all following sounds matchfish/dish; why/sigh; power/flower。
Assonancesame stressed vowel / vowel soundshake/hate; time/mind。不要和 consonance 混。
Consonant rhymesAlliteration / head rhymesame initial consonantsafe and sound; fish or fowl。
Consonancesame consonant sound, especially ending consonant in the course notesfish/dash; cold/bald。
Para-rhymeall consonants match but stressed vowel differsblock/black; reader/rider。
VisualEye rhymelooks similar in spelling but sounds differentblood/hood; daughter/laughter。
TypeMasculine rhymeends with a stressed syllablesmile/file; design/resign。
Feminine rhymeends with an unstressed syllablesmiling/filing; picky/tricky。
PositionEnd rhymerhyming words appear at the end of lines看行末。
Internal rhymerhyming words appear within a line or around a caesuraI met a man with a skin of tan。

4. Meter and Rhythm

逻辑链：Syllable → Foot → Meter → Rhythm。Meter 是由主要 foot 类型 + 每行 foot 数量共同命名的。
层级术语定义 / 记法例子
Foot typeIamb / iambic× / unstressed + stressedtoDAY; comPARE; iambic pentameter。
Foot typeTrochee / trochaic/ × stressed + unstressedENglish; TWINKle; trochaic tetrameter。
Foot typeDactyl / dactylic/ × × stressed + two unstressedACTually; NEgative。
Foot typeAnapaest / anapestic× × / two unstressed + stressedunderSTAND; contraDICT。
Foot typeSpondee / pyrrhicspondee = / /; pyrrhic = × ×了解即可。
Number of feetmono / di / tri / tetra / penta / hexa...1/2/3/4/5/6 feetpentameter = five feet; tetrameter = four feet。
MeterIambic pentameterfive iambs per line; usually ten syllablesShall I | compare | thee to | a sum | mer’s day?
MeterTrochaic tetrameterfour trochaic feet per lineTyger, Tyger, burning bright; Twinkle, twinkle, little star。

Metric variation

  • Deviation from expected meter.
  • Function: emphasis; avoid mechanical monotony.
  • Types: inversion, headless verse, catalexis.

Blank verse

Unrhymed iambic pentameter. 有格律、无押韵。Examples: Shakespeare, Milton.

Free verse

Written in lines, may be rhymed or unrhymed, but without regular metrical patterns. 核心是无固定格律。

6. Figure of Speech｜修辞手法

Function: figures of speech help make poetry visual, pictorial, concrete and specific by creating images. 答题时不要只写“用了 metaphor”，要继续说明它如何表现形象、情感、tone 或 theme。
手法定义例子 / 识别线索
Metaphor 隐喻Directly compares one thing to another without using “like” or “as”. 直接把甲说成乙。Time is a thief. / sunset of life. 看到 “A is B” 要警惕。
Simile 明喻Compares one thing to another using “like” or “as”. 用 like/as 明说相似性。Her smile is like the sun.
Personification 拟人Gives human qualities, feelings or actions to non-human things. 把非人事物写成人。The wind whispered through the trees. Dickinson 的 train poem 中 lap / lick / feed / neigh 把火车写成动物。
Metonymy 转喻Uses the name of something closely associated with the thing meant. 用相关物代替本体。The pen is mightier than the sword. pen = writing/ideas; sword = force/war. The White House issued a statement.
Synecdoche 提喻Uses a part to stand for the whole, or the whole for a part. 部分代整体，或整体代部分。All hands on deck! hands = sailors. They share the same roof. roof = house/family.
Irony 反语 / 反讽Says one thing but means the opposite, often for criticism, humor or sarcasm. 表面意思与真实意思相反。在暴风雨中说：What a lovely day!
Hyperbole / Overstatement 夸张Uses exaggerated language to intensify emotion or create comic/dramatic effect. 故意夸大。I have a million things to do today.
Understatement 低调陈述Deliberately makes something seem less serious or important than it really is. 故意说轻。重伤却说 It is just a scratch. 常制造 irony 或冷幽默。
Paradox 悖论An apparently contradictory statement that contains a basis of truth. 表面矛盾，但细想有真实意义。Less is more. / War is peace. / Freedom is slavery.
Oxymoron 矛盾修饰法Puts two contradictory words together to create a new meaning. 两个矛盾词放在一起。bittersweet, living dead, clever fool, true lies.
Pun 双关Uses similar sounds or multiple meanings of a word to create double meaning, humor or irony. 利用同音/多义。Time flies like an arrow; fruit flies like a banana. / Hamlet: “more than kin, and less than kind.”
Symbol / Symbolism 象征Uses a concrete object to stand for an abstract idea, value or emotion. 用具体事物代表抽象意义。A dove often symbolizes peace; the egg in The Egg can symbolize hope, fragility and failure.
Repetition 反复Repeats a word, phrase or line to emphasize an idea, rhythm or feeling. 重复以加强意义和节奏。And miles to go before I sleep, / And miles to go before I sleep.

最容易混的三组

  • Metaphor vs Simile: 有没有 like / as。
  • Metonymy vs Synecdoche: 相关物代替 vs 部分/整体代替。
  • Paradox vs Oxymoron: 一整个句子表面矛盾 vs 两个矛盾词并置。

The poet uses ______ to compare / personify / symbolize ______, making the image more concrete and revealing a tone of ______.

Drama｜戏剧

一页总览

Nature

Drama is both literary art and performing art. Western dramatic tradition originates in ancient Greece.

Aristotle’s six elements

Plot, character, thought, diction, music, spectacle.

Objective facts

  • Father of tragedy: Aeschylus.
  • Drama is action, not merely narration.
  • Tragedy uses pity and fear to produce purgation/catharsis.

1. Elements and Structure of Drama｜戏剧元素与结构

总览即可：Aristotle divides drama into six elements: Plot, Character, Thought, Diction, Spectacle, and Music. Structure usually refers to how the plot moves from exposition through complication to denouement / resolution.

Six elements｜六要素

  1. Plot 情节
  2. Character 人物
  3. Thought 思想
  4. Diction 语言
  5. Spectacle 视觉效果
  6. Music 声音 / 音乐

Basic structure｜基本结构

ExpositionComplicationReversal / ClimaxDenouement

这一点不用展开背，知道 drama 的结构围绕 conflict 的展开和解决即可。

2. Plot｜情节

Plot 核心：story 是按时间顺序排列的事件；plot 是带有因果关系和冲突发展的事件安排。PPT 里说 plot is the soul of tragedy。
术语定义功能 / 记法
Storya narrative of events arranged in time sequenceThe king died and then the queen died.
Plota narrative of events with the emphasis falling on causality; the way the story is toldThe king died and then the queen died of grief.
Expositionsets time, place, situation and characters; initiates the conflictmust be clear and quick; may introduce foreshadowing.
Foreshadowingearly clues inserted to make later action crediblebuilds suspense, creates tension, reveals character and helps complications develop.
Complicationdevelops the conflict initiated by exposition, providing it with depth and breadth不是一个点，而是冲突逐渐复杂化的一整段过程。
Denouement / Resolutionthe final part after climax where things fall into placeIdeally arises logically from exposition and complication.

Terms concerning plot｜和 complication 相关的情节术语

TermDefinition中文记法
Point of attackthe moment when the sense of balance is disturbed, or when an inciting force triggers the course of action交锋点 / 平衡被打破的点。
Rising actionaccumulation of incidents and development of character, carrying the conflict forward toward the climax动作上行；冲突越来越强。
Climaxthe high point of the plot; often the turning point高潮；冲突达到顶点。
Reversala turning point where the direction or fortune of action changes逆转；情节方向发生变化。
Anticlimaxany high point in the story after climax突降 / 反高潮；按 PPT 记：高潮之后的任何 high point。
Falling actionevents after the climax, moving downward toward the conclusion动作下行；高潮后的收束。
Subplot / Double plota secondary plot line that accompanies or parallels the main plot次要情节 / 双重情节。

3. Character｜人物

Character: any person appearing in a drama or narrative. 人物分析通常看角色在冲突中的位置，以及他/她是否复杂、是否变化。
TypeDefinition记法 / 例子
Protagonistthe main character in a drama; usually the most interesting and sympathetic figure involved in the central conflict主角；推动主要冲突的人物。
Antagonista character or force in conflict with the protagonist反角 / 对立力量；不一定是人，也可能是 society / nature。
Foila character who highlights another character’s traits through contrast or similarity陪衬人物；Laertes can act as a foil for Hamlet.
Round charactera well-developed, three-dimensional character; usually neither wholly good nor wholly evil, but convincingly human圆形人物；复杂、丰满、有深度。
Flat / Stock characteran underdeveloped or stereotypical character whose behavior or qualities conform to familiar dramatic conventions扁平 / 类型化人物；单一、固定、容易识别。

Characterization｜人物塑造

WayHow it works分析时怎么写
Appearanceportrays character through physical description, costume or visible details从外貌、衣着、状态看人物身份和处境。
Speechreveals personality, social position, emotion or attitude through what the character says and how they say it看语言风格、语气、称呼、沉默或重复。
Actionshows character through behavior, choices and reactions人物做了什么比“作者说他是什么样”更有说服力。
Others’ words / reactionsreveals a character through what others say about them and how others respond to them侧面描写；他人评价和反应帮助读者判断人物。

4. Thought｜思想 / 主题

Definition

Thought is the reasoning aspect of drama. It concerns a play’s theme, moral and symbolic meaning as a whole.

How to use it

  • 不要把 thought 理解成某个角色“脑子里的想法”。
  • 它更接近作品整体想表达的 meaning / theme。
  • 分析 Trifles 时可以写 gender inequality, women’s shared understanding, patriarchal blindness。

FeatureMeaning记法
Dynamicthe language of drama must advance the action戏剧语言要推动情节，不只是漂亮。
Purposefulselected to convey background information and characters’ thoughts and feelings台词同时交代背景和人物心理。
Oral and clearsuited for oral expression and immediately understood by the listener戏剧是要被听见的，所以要清楚。
Simple but interestingsimple and economical, but still interesting简洁但不能无聊。
Appropriatesuited to the character and the situation人物说话要符合身份、性格和场景。

6. Spectacle｜视觉效果

Definition

Spectacle refers to all visual aspects of production: scenery, lighting, costume, make-up and actors’ movement.

Function

It provides the psychological and physical environment and creates the atmosphere for the play.

For Trifles: the cold, gloomy kitchen helps create the atmosphere of isolation and oppression.

7. Music｜声音 / 音乐

Definition

Music refers to all auditory aspects of a play.

Function and examples

  • Function: to enhance the mood of the play.
  • Includes sound and silence.
  • Also includes the tonal pattern of the spoken word.

8. Genres of Drama｜戏剧类型

复习优先级：老师 PPT 重点讲 tragedy / comedy / tragicomedy / problem play，先背这四个；melodrama / farce / feminist theatre 放在后面，作为补充概念了解。

Tragedy｜悲剧

  • Definition: an imitation of a serious, complete action of certain magnitude.
  • Emotional effect: arouses pity and fear and produces katharsis / spiritual purgation.
  • Tragic protagonists: before mid-18th century they are often noble or high-born; in modern tragedy, they may be commonplace people who still show human greatness beyond the ordinary.
  • Tragic flaw: character traits leading to misjudgment and destruction.
  • Remember: tragedy is not merely sad; it is positive and optimistic in showing human dignity.

Comedy｜喜剧

  • Definition: a light, humorous play that ends happily.
  • Focus: shows how amusing, foolish or illogical people are.
  • Function: laughs at man’s follies, foibles and vices.
  • Effect: usually affirmative; leaves the audience pleased and gratified.
  • Contrast: tragedy shows human greatness; comedy shows social man and everyday failings.

Tragicomedy｜悲喜剧 / 正剧

  • Traditional sense: combines elements of tragedy and comedy.
  • Plot: serious action seems to lead to catastrophe, but an unexpected reversal brings a happy ending, e.g. Shakespeare’s The Tempest.
  • Modern sense: may be neither purely tragic nor purely comic, e.g. Waiting for Godot.
  • Purpose: shows the dual nature of reality where tragic and comic modes coexist.
  • Characteristics: often employs a romantic, fast-moving plot dealing with love, jealousy, disguises, treachery, intrigue and surprise, all moving toward a melodramatic resolution.

Problem Play｜问题剧

  • Definition: drama of social criticism developed in the 19th century, usually realistic.
  • Issues: discusses social, economic or political problems.
  • Method: presents debates between characters who represent conflicting points of view.
  • Purpose: to expose social ills and stimulate thought and discussion.
  • Names: Ibsen, Shaw, Alexandre Dumas fils are representative playwrights.

Melodrama｜情节剧（补充）

  • Definition: suspenseful and sensational drama appealing strongly to emotion.
  • Plot: often has clear good characters and bad characters; justice triumphs in a happy ending.
  • Purpose: to give the audience a thrill.
  • Feature: exaggerated, sensational, emotionally intense.
  • Warning: often good entertainment, but not valued for deep literary meaning.

Farce｜闹剧（补充）

  • Definition: a type of comedy based on highly exaggerated, extravagant and improbable situations.
  • Purpose: only aims to make the audience laugh.
  • Devices: physical humor, deliberate absurdity, bawdy jokes, drunkenness, mistaken identity.
  • Character: often one-dimensional characters in ludicrous situations.
  • Contrast: melodrama gives a thrill; farce gives a laugh.

Feminist Theatre｜女性主义戏剧（补充）

  • Focus: explores women’s oppression, rights and gender-related issues.
  • Background: women were excluded from dominant male cultural, social, sexual, political and intellectual discourses.
  • Goal: to subvert stereotyped “image of woman” and initiate positive change.
  • Strategies: exposes patriarchal prejudice, sexual oppression, wage inequities, discriminatory hiring and women’s invisibility.
  • Example link: Trifles can be read through gendered space, ignored women’s knowledge and female solidarity.

Genres quick comparison｜最容易混的几组

PairDifference一句话记法
Tragedy vs ComedyTragedy reveals human dignity through suffering; comedy exposes human folly through laughter and happy ending.Tragedy raises; comedy laughs.
Tragicomedy vs TragedyTragicomedy may begin seriously and move toward disaster, but ends happily through reversal.Serious action + happy ending.
Problem Play vs Feminist TheatreProblem play targets broad social issues through debate; feminist theatre specifically foregrounds women’s oppression and gender power.Problem play = social debate; Feminist theatre = gender politics.
Melodrama vs Farce（补充）Melodrama appeals to emotion and suspense; farce relies on absurdity and exaggeration for laughter.Melodrama = thrill; Farce = laugh.

Drama 最可能考法

  1. 客观题：origin, Aeschylus, six elements；genres 优先 tragedy / comedy / tragicomedy / problem play，melodrama / farce 作为补充。
  2. 辨析：story vs plot; tragedy vs comedy; melodrama vs farce。
  3. 作品分析：Trifles 的 title irony, gendered space, women’s evidence, sisterhood。

Novel / Fiction｜小说

一页总览

Fiction

A general term for invented stories. Modern fiction includes short story, novella, novel, romance, fable and other prose narratives.

Novel

An extended prose narrative about largely fictional but usually realistic characters and plausible events.

Elements of Novel

  • Plot: events arranged through conflict and causality.
  • Characterization: how characters are presented and developed.
  • Theme: the central meaning, not merely the subject.
  • Style: how the story is written.
  • Point of View: the most technical aspect of novel analysis.
  • Setting: time, place and environment.

Short-answer Questions｜辨析题

答题公式：X refers to ______, whereas Y refers to ______. The key distinction lies in ______. In literary analysis, this distinction helps us examine ______.

A 级：优先背熟

Sex vs Gender

Sex is biological; gender is social and cultural. Biology determines sex, whereas culture constructs gender roles and expectations.

Direct vs Indirect Characterization

Direct characterization tells the reader what a character is like; indirect characterization lets the reader infer personality through speech, action, behavior and reactions.

Round vs Flat Character

Round characters are complex and multidimensional; flat characters are one-dimensional and defined by limited traits. 核心维度是 complexity。

Story vs Plot

Story is chronological sequence; plot emphasizes causality. “The king died and then the queen died” is story; “of grief” makes it plot.

Modernism / Modernity / Modernization

Modernism is an artistic/literary movement; modernity is an ideology or condition rooted in capitalist/Enlightenment thought; modernization is institutional transformation in society, economy, city, industry and education.

B 级：概念清楚即可

Blank Verse vs Free Verse

Blank verse has meter but no rhyme: unrhymed iambic pentameter. Free verse lacks regular metrical pattern and may be rhymed or unrhymed.

End-stopped vs Run-on Line

End-stopped line completes syntax/meaning at the line ending; run-on line / enjambment carries meaning into the next line. 核心维度是行末是否完成。

Alliteration / Assonance / Consonance

Alliteration = initial consonant; assonance = vowel sound; consonance = consonant sound, especially non-initial or ending consonant in the course examples.

Tragedy vs Comedy

Tragedy deals with serious action, human dignity and purgation through pity/fear; comedy ends happily and exposes human follies in social life.

Melodrama vs Farce

Melodrama gives a thrill through sensational emotion; farce gives a laugh through exaggerated, improbable situations.

Analytical Essay｜赏析题模板

赏析题核心：写成 short comprehensive essay。每段都要有 evidence → device → effect → theme。

Poetry essay route

  1. Introduction: author, title, genre, central theme.
  2. Form: stanza, rhyme scheme, meter, enjambment/caesura.
  3. Diction, imagery & figures of speech: explain concrete words, sensory images and rhetorical effects.
  4. Voice & tone: speaker’s attitude and how it is produced.
  5. Conclusion: connect form and meaning.

In “[title],” [author] explores ______ through a combination of form, diction, imagery and voice. The poem’s ______ form creates ______, while the image/phrase “______” suggests ______. The speaker’s ______ tone reveals ______. Therefore, the poem turns ______ into a broader reflection on ______.

Fiction / drama essay route

  1. Introduction: genre, situation, central conflict/theme.
  2. Plot / structure: how conflict develops.
  3. Characterization: direct/indirect, round/flat, change.
  4. Style / symbol: diction, dialogue, irony, title, objects.
  5. Conclusion: how literary techniques deepen the theme.

[Title] presents ______ through the conflict between ______ and ______. Through ______, the author reveals ______. The symbol of “______” develops from a concrete object into an image of ______. Taken together, these elements expose / criticize / affirm ______.

Key Works｜重点作品

使用方法：每篇作品先背“主题 + 2个手法 + 2个证据词/物象 + 1句英文收束”。赏析题不要只背中文观点。

William Blake, “London”

Core

Urban suffering, oppression, institutional corruption, loss of innocence.

Evidence

“charter’d”; “every”; “mind-forg’d manacles”; “black’ning Church”; “Marriage hearse”.

Form / Tone

Four quatrains; ABAB; regular form contrasts with social misery; tone is gloomy, critical and indignant.

Through the repeated word “every,” Blake presents suffering as universal rather than accidental.

William Shakespeare, “Sonnet 130”

Core

Realistic love; anti-Petrarchan convention; sincerity over false comparison.

Evidence

“nothing like the sun”; “black wires”; “I love to hear her speak”; final couplet.

Form / Tone

Shakespearean sonnet: abab cdcd efef gg; iambic pentameter; ironic yet affectionate tone.

The speaker rejects conventional comparisons not to insult his mistress, but to expose the artificiality of exaggerated love poetry.

Emily Dickinson, “I like to see it lap the Miles”

Core

Train as animal-like machine; industrial power; fascination and unease.

Evidence

lap, lick, feed, peer, neigh; “docile and omnipotent”.

Device

Personification is the key figure; motion and sound imagery animate the train.

By giving the train animal actions, Dickinson turns industrial machinery into a living presence.

Robert Browning, “Soliloquy of the Spanish Cloister”

Core

A jealous monk exposes his own hypocrisy and hatred toward Brother Lawrence.

Form

Nine eight-line stanzas; rough trochaic tetrameter; spoken, colloquial language.

Voice / Tone

Speaker talks to himself; sarcastic, contemptuous tone; self-exposure and psychological portrayal.

The poem’s dramatic voice reveals more about the speaker’s moral corruption than about the person he attacks.

Susan Glaspell, Trifles

Core

Patriarchy, gendered knowledge, domestic space, female solidarity.

Evidence

kitchen, quilt, birdcage, dead bird, knot; men dismiss women’s observations as trifles.

Irony

The “trifles” become the crucial evidence that reveals Minnie Wright’s suffering.

The title is ironic because the objects dismissed as trivial contain the emotional and legal truth of the play.

Sherwood Anderson, “The Egg”

Core

American Dream, failure, absurd ambition, family pressure.

Symbol

The egg suggests hope and new life, but also fragility, failure and the repetitive cycle of disappointment.

Character / POV

First-person observer; father is rounder and more active; irony and wry humor.

The egg becomes a symbol of fragile hope: it promises life and success, but repeatedly breaks into failure.

Shakespeare, A Midsummer Night’s Dream

Basic ID

Romantic comedy; love, dream, illusion, imagination, transformation and reconciliation.

Form

Three interlocking plots; forest as a space where ordinary rules are suspended.

Priority

低于六个核心文本；保留作品识别、情节结构和主题分析即可。
