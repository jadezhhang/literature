1. Classification of Poetry｜诗歌分类

核心：Poetry can be roughly classified into narrative poetry, lyric poetry, and dramatic poetry. 客观题一般考“某种诗属于哪一类”，赏析题则用它判断说话方式和文本功能。

Narrative poetry｜叙事诗

  • Epic: long narrative poem about cultural heroes and grand adventures; e.g. Beowulf.
  • Ballad: folk narrative poem, often in quatrains with simple colloquial language; e.g. Robin Hood ballads.
  • Romance: medieval fiction in verse or prose about chivalry, adventure and love; e.g. Sir Gawain and the Green Knight.

Lyric poetry｜抒情诗

  • Expresses personal feeling, meditation, or a concentrated moment of emotion.
  • Common forms: epigram, elegy, ode, aubade, sestina, sonnet, villanelle.
  • Exam clue: usually focuses on speaker, tone, imagery, diction and feeling rather than plot.

Dramatic poetry｜戏剧诗

  • Presented through a speaker, persona, or dramatic situation.
  • Dramatic monologue: an implied audience, no real dialogue; speaker reveals the self through speech.
  • Dramatic soliloquy: a character speaks aloud, often revealing inner thought.

Lyric Forms Quick Guide｜抒情诗形式速记

Intro note: Lyric poetry mainly expresses personal feelings, meditation, or a concentrated moment of emotion rather than telling a complete story. The following forms are useful for objective questions and term recognition.

Epigram｜警句诗

Objective Questions Term Recognition

Core meaning: A very short and witty poem or statement, often with a sharp, ironic, or surprising ending.

Exam clue: Look for words like short, witty, concise, pointed, satirical.

Memory hook: Epigram = short + witty + punchline.

Elegy｜挽歌

Objective Questions Term Recognition

Core meaning: A poem of mourning, usually written for someone who has died or for a serious loss.

Exam clue: Look for death, grief, loss, mourning, memory, reflection.

Memory hook: Elegy = mourning poem.

Ode｜颂歌

Objective Questions Term Recognition

Core meaning: A serious and elevated lyric poem that praises a person, object, place, or abstract idea.

Exam clue: Look for praise, admiration, dignity, elevated language.

Memory hook: Ode = praise poem.

Aubade｜晨歌

Objective Questions Term Recognition

Core meaning: A morning poem, often about dawn, lovers parting, or the sadness of separation at daybreak.

Exam clue: Look for morning, dawn, daybreak, lovers’ separation.

Memory hook: Aubade = morning + separation.

Sestina｜六节诗

Objective Questions Term Recognition

Core meaning: A fixed poetic form built on repeated end words in a strict pattern.

Exam clue: Focus on structure and repeated end words, not theme.

Memory hook: Sestina = six + repeated end words.

Sonnet｜十四行诗

Objective Questions Term Recognition

Core meaning: A 14-line lyric poem, often about love, beauty, time, or reflection.

Exam clue: Look for 14 lines, Shakespearean sonnet, Petrarchan sonnet, rhyme scheme, iambic pentameter.

Memory hook: Sonnet = 14-line lyric poem.

Villanelle｜维拉内拉体

Objective Questions Term Recognition

Core meaning: A fixed 19-line poem with repeated lines and a circular structure.

Exam clue: Look for refrain, repetition, fixed form, obsessive emotion.

Memory hook: Villanelle = repetition + obsession.

Exam Shortcut｜考试速记
Epigram = short and witty. Elegy = mourning. Ode = praise. Aubade = morning separation. Sestina = repeated end words. Sonnet = 14 lines. Villanelle = repeated refrains.

2. Stanza / Rhyme Scheme / Line Movement

Stanza 看行数；rhyme scheme 看每行末尾的押韵模式；line movement 看句意是否在行末完成。
行数 / 形式名称识别点
2 linesCouplet / distich双行诗；heroic couplet 常见 aa bb cc。
3 linesTercet / triplet三行诗；terza rima 用 aba bcb cdc。
4 linesQuatrain四行诗节；ballad 常用 abcb 或 abab。
5/6/7/8 linesQuintain / sextain / septet / octave记名称即可；octave 常与 Petrarchan sonnet 相关。
9 linesSpenserian stanzaababbcbcc。
14 linesSonnet通常与 iambic pentameter、固定 rhyme scheme 一起考。

High-frequency rhyme schemes

  • Heroic couplet: aa bb cc...
  • Ballad: abcb / abab.
  • Terza rima: aba bcb cdc...
  • Ottava rima: abababcc.
  • Spenserian stanza: ababbcbcc.

Sonnet forms

  • Shakespearean / English sonnet: abab cdcd efef gg.
  • Petrarchan / Italian sonnet: octave + sestet; often abbaabba cde/cdc patterns.
  • Spenserian sonnet: abab bcbc cdcd ee.

End-stopped line

A line whose syntactic unit or sense unit is completed at the line ending. 行末意思较完整，停顿较明显。

Run-on line / Enjambment

The sentence or poetic argument runs beyond the line ending without a full pause or punctuation. 意思延续到下一行，行末停顿较短。

3. Rhyme 与 Sound

Rhyme: the formalized consonance of syllables. 判断时先看 stressed vowel，再看 following sounds / consonants。

维度术语核心定义例子 / 易错点
Vowel rhymesFull / perfect / exact rhymestressed vowel and all following sounds matchfish/dish; why/sigh; power/flower。
Assonancesame stressed vowel / vowel soundshake/hate; time/mind。不要和 consonance 混。
Consonant rhymesAlliteration / head rhymesame initial consonantsafe and sound; fish or fowl。
Consonancesame consonant sound, especially ending consonant in the course notesfish/dash; cold/bald。
Para-rhymeall consonants match but stressed vowel differsblock/black; reader/rider。
VisualEye rhymelooks similar in spelling but sounds differentblood/hood; daughter/laughter。
TypeMasculine rhymeends with a stressed syllablesmile/file; design/resign。
Feminine rhymeends with an unstressed syllablesmiling/filing; picky/tricky。
PositionEnd rhymerhyming words appear at the end of lines看行末。
Internal rhymerhyming words appear within a line or around a caesuraI met a man with a skin of tan。

4. Meter and Rhythm

逻辑链：Syllable → Foot → Meter → Rhythm。Meter 是由主要 foot 类型 + 每行 foot 数量共同命名的。
层级术语定义 / 记法例子
Foot typeIamb / iambic× / unstressed + stressedtoDAY; comPARE; iambic pentameter。
Foot typeTrochee / trochaic/ × stressed + unstressedENglish; TWINKle; trochaic tetrameter。
Foot typeDactyl / dactylic/ × × stressed + two unstressedACTually; NEgative。
Foot typeAnapaest / anapestic× × / two unstressed + stressedunderSTAND; contraDICT。
Foot typeSpondee / pyrrhicspondee = / /; pyrrhic = × ×了解即可。
Number of feetmono / di / tri / tetra / penta / hexa...1/2/3/4/5/6 feetpentameter = five feet; tetrameter = four feet。
MeterIambic pentameterfive iambs per line; usually ten syllablesShall I | compare | thee to | a sum | mer’s day?
MeterTrochaic tetrameterfour trochaic feet per lineTyger, Tyger, burning bright; Twinkle, twinkle, little star。

Metric variation

  • Deviation from expected meter.
  • Function: emphasis; avoid mechanical monotony.
  • Types: inversion, headless verse, catalexis.

Blank verse

Unrhymed iambic pentameter. 有格律、无押韵。Examples: Shakespeare, Milton.

Free verse

Written in lines, may be rhymed or unrhymed, but without regular metrical patterns. 核心是无固定格律。

6. Figure of Speech｜修辞手法

Function: figures of speech help make poetry visual, pictorial, concrete and specific by creating images. 答题时不要只写“用了 metaphor”，要继续说明它如何表现形象、情感、tone 或 theme。
手法定义例子 / 识别线索
Metaphor 隐喻Directly compares one thing to another without using “like” or “as”. 直接把甲说成乙。Time is a thief. / sunset of life. 看到 “A is B” 要警惕。
Simile 明喻Compares one thing to another using “like” or “as”. 用 like/as 明说相似性。Her smile is like the sun.
Personification 拟人Gives human qualities, feelings or actions to non-human things. 把非人事物写成人。The wind whispered through the trees. Dickinson 的 train poem 中 lap / lick / feed / neigh 把火车写成动物。
Metonymy 转喻Uses the name of something closely associated with the thing meant. 用相关物代替本体。The pen is mightier than the sword. pen = writing/ideas; sword = force/war. The White House issued a statement.
Synecdoche 提喻Uses a part to stand for the whole, or the whole for a part. 部分代整体，或整体代部分。All hands on deck! hands = sailors. They share the same roof. roof = house/family.
Irony 反语 / 反讽Says one thing but means the opposite, often for criticism, humor or sarcasm. 表面意思与真实意思相反。在暴风雨中说：What a lovely day!
Hyperbole / Overstatement 夸张Uses exaggerated language to intensify emotion or create comic/dramatic effect. 故意夸大。I have a million things to do today.
Understatement 低调陈述Deliberately makes something seem less serious or important than it really is. 故意说轻。重伤却说 It is just a scratch. 常制造 irony 或冷幽默。
Paradox 悖论An apparently contradictory statement that contains a basis of truth. 表面矛盾，但细想有真实意义。Less is more. / War is peace. / Freedom is slavery.
Oxymoron 矛盾修饰法Puts two contradictory words together to create a new meaning. 两个矛盾词放在一起。bittersweet, living dead, clever fool, true lies.
Pun 双关Uses similar sounds or multiple meanings of a word to create double meaning, humor or irony. 利用同音/多义。Time flies like an arrow; fruit flies like a banana. / Hamlet: “more than kin, and less than kind.”
Symbol / Symbolism 象征Uses a concrete object to stand for an abstract idea, value or emotion. 用具体事物代表抽象意义。A dove often symbolizes peace; the egg in The Egg can symbolize hope, fragility and failure.
Repetition 反复Repeats a word, phrase or line to emphasize an idea, rhythm or feeling. 重复以加强意义和节奏。And miles to go before I sleep, / And miles to go before I sleep.

最容易混的三组

  • Metaphor vs Simile: 有没有 like / as。
  • Metonymy vs Synecdoche: 相关物代替 vs 部分/整体代替。
  • Paradox vs Oxymoron: 一整个句子表面矛盾 vs 两个矛盾词并置。

Objective facts

1. Elements and Structure of Drama｜戏剧元素与结构

总览即可：Aristotle divides drama into six elements: Plot, Character, Thought, Diction, Spectacle, and Music. Structure usually refers to how the plot moves from exposition through complication to denouement / resolution.

Six elements｜六要素

  1. Plot 情节
  2. Character 人物
  3. Thought 思想
  4. Diction 语言
  5. Spectacle 视觉效果
  6. Music 声音 / 音乐

Basic structure｜基本结构

ExpositionComplicationReversal / ClimaxDenouement

这一点不用展开背，知道 drama 的结构围绕 conflict 的展开和解决即可。

2. Plot｜情节

Plot 核心：story 是按时间顺序排列的事件；plot 是带有因果关系和冲突发展的事件安排。PPT 里说 plot is the soul of tragedy。
术语定义功能 / 记法
Storya narrative of events arranged in time sequenceThe king died and then the queen died.
Plota narrative of events with the emphasis falling on causality; the way the story is toldThe king died and then the queen died of grief.
Expositionsets time, place, situation and characters; initiates the conflictmust be clear and quick; may introduce foreshadowing.
Foreshadowingearly clues inserted to make later action crediblebuilds suspense, creates tension, reveals character and helps complications develop.
Complicationdevelops the conflict initiated by exposition, providing it with depth and breadth不是一个点，而是冲突逐渐复杂化的一整段过程。
Denouement / Resolutionthe final part after climax where things fall into placeIdeally arises logically from exposition and complication.

Terms concerning plot｜和 complication 相关的情节术语

TermDefinition中文记法
Point of attackthe moment when the sense of balance is disturbed, or when an inciting force triggers the course of action交锋点 / 平衡被打破的点。
Rising actionaccumulation of incidents and development of character, carrying the conflict forward toward the climax动作上行；冲突越来越强。
Climaxthe high point of the plot; often the turning point高潮；冲突达到顶点。
Reversala turning point where the direction or fortune of action changes逆转；情节方向发生变化。
Anticlimaxany high point in the story after climax突降 / 反高潮；按 PPT 记：高潮之后的任何 high point。

3. Character｜人物

Character: any person appearing in a drama or narrative. 人物分析通常看角色在冲突中的位置，以及他/她是否复杂、是否变化。
TypeDefinition记法 / 例子
Protagonistthe main character in a drama; usually the most interesting and sympathetic figure involved in the central conflict主角；推动主要冲突的人物。
Antagonista character or force in conflict with the protagonist反角 / 对立力量；不一定是人，也可能是 society / nature。
Foila character who highlights another character’s traits through contrast or similarity陪衬人物；Laertes can act as a foil for Hamlet.
Round charactera well-developed, three-dimensional character; usually neither wholly good nor wholly evil, but convincingly human圆形人物；复杂、丰满、有深度。
Flat / Stock characteran underdeveloped or stereotypical character whose behavior or qualities conform to familiar dramatic conventions扁平 / 类型化人物；单一、固定、容易识别。

WayHow it works分析时怎么写

4. Thought｜思想 / 主题

Definition

Thought is the reasoning aspect of drama. It concerns a play’s theme, moral and symbolic meaning as a whole.

How to use it

  • 不要把 thought 理解成某个角色“脑子里的想法”。
  • 它更接近作品整体想表达的 meaning / theme。
  • 分析 Trifles 时可以写 gender inequality, women’s shared understanding, patriarchal blindness。

6. Spectacle｜视觉效果

Definition

Spectacle refers to all visual aspects of production: scenery, lighting, costume, make-up and actors’ movement.

Function

It provides the psychological and physical environment and creates the atmosphere for the play.

For Trifles: the cold, gloomy kitchen helps create the atmosphere of isolation and oppression.

7. Music｜声音 / 音乐

Definition

Music refers to all auditory aspects of a play.

Function and examples

  • Function: to enhance the mood of the play.
  • Includes sound and silence.
  • Also includes the tonal pattern of the spoken word.

8. Genres of Drama｜戏剧类型

复习优先级：老师 PPT 重点讲 tragedy / comedy / tragicomedy / problem play，先背这四个；melodrama / farce / feminist theatre 放在后面，作为补充概念了解。

Tragedy｜悲剧

  • Definition: an imitation of a serious, complete action of certain magnitude.
  • Emotional effect: arouses pity and fear and produces katharsis / spiritual purgation.
  • Tragic protagonists: before mid-18th century they are often noble or high-born; in modern tragedy, they may be commonplace people who still show human greatness beyond the ordinary.
  • Tragic flaw: character traits leading to misjudgment and destruction.
  • Remember: tragedy is not merely sad; it is positive and optimistic in showing human dignity.

Comedy｜喜剧

  • Definition: a light, humorous play that ends happily.
  • Focus: shows how amusing, foolish or illogical people are.
  • Function: laughs at man’s follies, foibles and vices.
  • Effect: usually affirmative; leaves the audience pleased and gratified.
  • Contrast: tragedy shows human greatness; comedy shows social man and everyday failings.

Tragicomedy｜悲喜剧 / 正剧

  • Traditional sense: combines elements of tragedy and comedy.
  • Plot: serious action seems to lead to catastrophe, but an unexpected reversal brings a happy ending, e.g. Shakespeare’s The Tempest.
  • Modern sense: may be neither purely tragic nor purely comic, e.g. Waiting for Godot.
  • Purpose: shows the dual nature of reality where tragic and comic modes coexist.
  • Characteristics: often employs a romantic, fast-moving plot dealing with love, jealousy, disguises, treachery, intrigue and surprise, all moving toward a melodramatic resolution.

Problem Play｜问题剧

  • Definition: drama of social criticism developed in the 19th century, usually realistic.
  • Issues: discusses social, economic or political problems.
  • Method: presents debates between characters who represent conflicting points of view.
  • Purpose: to expose social ills and stimulate thought and discussion.
  • Names: Ibsen, Shaw, Alexandre Dumas fils are representative playwrights.

Melodrama｜情节剧（补充）

  • Definition: suspenseful and sensational drama appealing strongly to emotion.
  • Plot: often has clear good characters and bad characters; justice triumphs in a happy ending.
  • Purpose: to give the audience a thrill.
  • Feature: exaggerated, sensational, emotionally intense.
  • Warning: often good entertainment, but not valued for deep literary meaning.

Farce｜闹剧（补充）

  • Definition: a type of comedy based on highly exaggerated, extravagant and improbable situations.
  • Purpose: only aims to make the audience laugh.
  • Devices: physical humor, deliberate absurdity, bawdy jokes, drunkenness, mistaken identity.
  • Character: often one-dimensional characters in ludicrous situations.
  • Contrast: melodrama gives a thrill; farce gives a laugh.

Feminist Theatre｜女性主义戏剧（补充）

  • Focus: explores women’s oppression, rights and gender-related issues.
  • Background: women were excluded from dominant male cultural, social, sexual, political and intellectual discourses.
  • Goal: to subvert stereotyped “image of woman” and initiate positive change.
  • Strategies: exposes patriarchal prejudice, sexual oppression, wage inequities, discriminatory hiring and women’s invisibility.
  • Example link: Trifles can be read through gendered space, ignored women’s knowledge and female solidarity.

Genres quick comparison｜最容易混的几组

PairDifference一句话记法
Tragedy vs ComedyTragedy reveals human dignity through suffering; comedy exposes human folly through laughter and happy ending.Tragedy raises; comedy laughs.
Tragicomedy vs TragedyTragicomedy may begin seriously and move toward disaster, but ends happily through reversal.Serious action + happy ending.
Problem Play vs Feminist TheatreProblem play targets broad social issues through debate; feminist theatre specifically foregrounds women’s oppression and gender power.Problem play = social debate; Feminist theatre = gender politics.
Melodrama vs Farce（补充）Melodrama appeals to emotion and suspense; farce relies on absurdity and exaggeration for laughter.Melodrama = thrill; Farce = laugh.

  2. 辨析：story vs plot; tragedy vs comedy; melodrama vs farce。

Novel / Fiction｜小说

一页总览

Fiction

A general term for invented stories. Modern fiction includes short story, novella, novel, romance, fable and other prose narratives.

Novel

An extended prose narrative about largely fictional but usually realistic characters and plausible events.

Elements of Novel

  • Plot: events arranged through conflict and causality.
  • Characterization: representation of characters and the ways they are presented.
  • Theme: the central meaning, not merely the subject.
  • Style: the verbal identity of the work.
  • Point of View: the most technical aspect of novel analysis.
  • Setting: time, place and environment.

3. Point of View｜叙述视角

核心：POV 不是单纯“第几人称”，而是控制 narrator / character / reader 之间的信息量，用来制造不同的 literary effect。

Narrator｜叙述者：who is saying?

  • First-person narrator: narrator is a participant, using I / me / my / we / us / our.
  • Can be as protagonist（自己讲自己的故事）or as non-protagonist / observer（旁观者讲别人的故事）.
  • Second-person narrator: narratee as a participant, using you / your.
  • Third-person narrator: narrator is non-participant, using he / she / they or character names.

Narrative point of view｜who is seeing?

It means the position through which events are observed and presented. 重点不是“谁开口”，而是读者通过谁的视角看到故事。

  • First-person view: I / we 的视角，信息受 narrator 自身经验限制。
  • Second-person view: you / your，较少见，读者像被拉进故事。
  • Third-person view: he / she / they，narrator 不直接参与故事。
  • Alternating / multiple person view: 叙述人称或视角发生转换。

Third-person point of view｜第三人称视角三类

Type核心定义Information relation识别方法Example / 例子
Third-person omniscient
全知视角		Narrator is all-knowing and can enter the minds of more than one character.Zero focalization: narrator > character.能自由知道多个角色的 thoughts / feelings，也可跨地点、时间、人物。Hardy, Tess of the D’Urbervilles; Leo Tolstoy, Anna Karenina.
Third-person limited

Third-person objective

4. Characterization｜人物塑造

Characterization：the representation of a character, to establish each character as somewhat unique. 也就是作品怎样让人物显得有自己的特点。

Direct characterization｜直接描写

The narrator or a character directly tells the reader another character’s appearance or personality.

  • 关键词：telling。
  • 常见内容：直接说明外貌、性格、身份、背景。
  • 作用：快速提供 background information。

Indirect characterization｜间接描写

Narrators or characters describe what another character is like without direct comment.

  • 关键词：showing。
  • 常见方式：appearance, speech, action, thoughts, and other characters’ comments or reactions.
  • 作用：让读者通过细节 infer 人物性格，给读者留下判断空间。

Short-answer Questions｜辨析题

答题公式：X refers to ______, whereas Y refers to ______. The key distinction lies in ______. In literary analysis, this distinction helps us examine ______.

A 级：优先背熟

Sex vs Gender

Sex is biological; gender is social and cultural. Biology determines sex, whereas culture constructs gender roles and expectations.

Direct vs Indirect Characterization

Direct characterization tells the reader what a character is like; indirect characterization lets the reader infer personality through speech, action, behavior and reactions.

Round vs Flat Character

Round characters are complex and multidimensional; flat characters are one-dimensional and defined by limited traits.

Story vs Plot

Story is a narrative of events arranged in their time sequence; plot is a narrative of events with the emphasis falling on causality.

Modernism / Modernity / Modernization

Modernism is an artistic/literary movement; modernity is an ideology or condition rooted in capitalist/Enlightenment thought; modernization is institutional transformation in society, economy, city, industry and education.

B 级：概念清楚即可

Blank Verse vs Free Verse

Blank verse has meter but no rhyme: unrhymed iambic pentameter. Free verse lacks regular metrical pattern and may be rhymed or unrhymed.

End-stopped vs Run-on Line

End-stopped line completes syntax/meaning at the line ending; run-on line / enjambment carries meaning into the next line. 核心维度是行末是否完成。

Tone vs Diction

Tone refers to the writer’s or speaker’s attitude toward the subject, characters, or audience; diction refers to the writer’s choice and use of words.

Alliteration / Assonance / Consonance

Alliteration = repetition of initial consonant sounds in nearby words.

Assonance = repetition of vowel sounds in nearby words.

Consonance = repetition of consonant sounds in nearby words, not limited to the beginning; it often appears in the middle or at the end of words.

Tragedy vs Comedy

Tragedy deals with serious action, human dignity and purgation through pity/fear; comedy ends happily and exposes human follies in social life.

Melodrama vs Farce

Melodrama gives a thrill through sensational emotion; farce gives a laugh through exaggerated, improbable situations.

Protagonist vs Antagonist

Protagonist is the central character; antagonist is the opposing character or force.

Omniscient POV vs Limited omniscient POV

Omniscient POV is all-knowing and can reveal the thoughts and feelings of more than one character. Limited omniscient POV is restricted to one character and reveals only that character’s inner world.

Brief Analysis Questions｜简析题重点题库

Current priority: Only London and I like to see it lap the Miles are current priorities for brief analysis questions. Other works are mainly for objective questions or analytical essay preparation.
答题方式：简析题围绕具体作品/片段回答，答案控制在 80–130 words 左右。写法：文本细节 → 手法 → 效果 → 主题。

Part A. London｜William Blake

Question 1: How do imagery and diction contribute to the theme of London?

In London, Blake uses dark imagery and oppressive diction to present a city full of suffering and control. Images such as “charter’d street,” “charter’d Thames,” “marks of weakness,” “mind-forg’d manacles,” “black’ning Church,” and “Marriage hearse” create a gloomy picture of urban life. The word “charter’d” suggests restriction and control, while “manacles” suggests psychological imprisonment. The repetition of painful words such as “cry,” “fear,” “blood,” “curse,” and “hearse” strengthens the atmosphere of misery. Through these images and word choices, Blake criticizes the social, political, and religious institutions that oppress the people of London.

Question 2: What does “mind-forg’d manacles” mean in London?

“Mind-forg’d manacles” is one of the central metaphors in London. “Manacles” are chains, so the phrase suggests imprisonment and lack of freedom. However, these chains are “mind-forg’d,” which means they are made in the mind. Therefore, the oppression in the poem is not only physical, social, or political, but also psychological. People in London have internalized the restrictions imposed by society, religion, law, and power. They suffer not only because they are controlled from outside, but also because their own minds have been shaped by fear, obedience, and despair.

Question 3: What is the function of repetition in London?

Repetition plays an important role in London. The repeated use of “every” in the second stanza emphasizes the universality of suffering. The speaker hears pain in “every cry,” “every voice,” and “every ban,” suggesting that misery affects all groups of people, including men, infants, chimney-sweepers, soldiers, and young women. The repetition of “marks” in “marks of weakness, marks of woe” also shows that suffering is visibly printed on people’s faces. Through repetition, Blake creates a sense of inescapable oppression and shows that London’s misery is not individual but social and systematic.

Question 4: Analyze the meaning of “Marriage hearse” in London.

“Marriage hearse” is an oxymoron that combines two opposite ideas: marriage and death. Marriage usually suggests love, union, family, and new life, while a hearse is connected with death and funerals. By putting these two words together, Blake shows that even marriage and family life have been corrupted by poverty, disease, prostitution, and social oppression. The phrase also suggests the destruction of innocence and hope across generations. In this way, “Marriage hearse” becomes a powerful image of a society where love, life, and family are shadowed by death and suffering.

Part B. I like to see it lap the Miles｜Emily Dickinson

Question 1: How is the train described in I like to see it lap the Miles?

In I like to see it lap the Miles, Dickinson describes the train through personification and animal imagery. Although the train is never directly named, it is presented as a living creature that can “lap,” “lick,” “feed,” “crawl,” “complain,” “chase itself,” and “neigh.” These verbs make the machine seem energetic, powerful, and almost animal-like. The train moves through valleys, mountains, roads, quarries, and hills before stopping at its “stable door.” By turning the train into a living creature, Dickinson makes modern industrial power vivid, dynamic, and slightly mysterious.

Question 2: What is the effect of personification in the poem?

Personification is the most important technique in I like to see it lap the Miles. Dickinson gives the train animal-like actions: it “laps” miles, “licks” valleys, “feeds” at tanks, “crawls,” “complains,” and “neighs.” These descriptions transform the train from a machine into a powerful living creature. This makes the train more vivid and dramatic, allowing readers to feel its movement, energy, and sound. At the same time, personification suggests the strange power of industrial technology. The train seems both controlled and uncontrollable, both “docile” and “omnipotent.”

Question 3: What is Dickinson’s attitude toward the train?

Dickinson’s attitude toward the train is mixed. On the one hand, she seems fascinated by its power, speed, and movement. Words such as “prodigious” and “omnipotent” suggest admiration and awe. The train is presented as a remarkable product of human ingenuity. On the other hand, words such as “supercilious,” “horrid,” and “hooting” create a sense of uneasiness. The train appears almost monstrous, noisy, and too powerful. Therefore, the poem does not simply celebrate industrialization. It presents the train as both impressive and disturbing, showing the poet’s complex response to modern technology.

Question 4: How do sound and movement contribute to the poem’s meaning?

Sound and movement are central to I like to see it lap the Miles. The poem uses energetic verbs such as “lap,” “lick,” “step,” “crawl,” “chase,” and “neigh” to create a sense of continuous motion. The repetition of “And” helps the poem move forward, imitating the train’s progress along the tracks. Sound words such as “complaining,” “hooting,” and “neigh” evoke the noise of the engine and whistle. Enjambment also pushes the reader from one line to the next, echoing the train’s movement. Through sound and rhythm, Dickinson makes the train feel alive and powerful.

The Egg｜Sherwood Anderson

Analytical Essay Highest priority

1. Main Theme｜主旨

The Egg is a short story about failure, ambition, and the American Dream. Its plot seems simple and uneventful. The father first runs a chicken farm, then opens a restaurant, and finally tries to entertain a customer with eggs. However, all these attempts end in failure. Through these repeated failures, Anderson shows that the dream of “getting up in the world” is fragile and painful for ordinary people.

2. Artistic Techniques｜艺术技巧

The most important technique in the story is characterization. The father is not just a flat comic figure. At first, he is an ordinary man, but after marriage and family responsibility, he begins to want success. His desire to rise in the world gradually changes him. He becomes ambitious, anxious, and finally desperate. In the scene with Joe Kane, his actions and gestures show his embarrassment and sense of failure. The egg is also a central symbol. It represents hope and the American Dream, but it is also fragile, uncooperative, and easily broken. This creates irony: the more the father tries to control the egg and prove himself, the more powerless he becomes.

3. Significance｜影响 / 意义

In my view, the story does not simply criticize hard work. Instead, it questions the pressure behind the American Dream. The father fails not only because of personal weakness, but also because he believes too strongly in the idea that everyone must succeed and move upward. His tragedy reminds us that success should not be the only value of life.

Trifles｜Susan Glaspell

Analytical Essay Highest priority

1. Main Theme｜主旨

Trifles is a one-act play about a murder investigation, but its real focus is gender inequality and women’s hidden suffering. John Wright has been murdered, and his wife Minnie Wright is suspected. The men enter the house to look for legal evidence, while Mrs. Hale and Mrs. Peters notice small domestic details in the kitchen. These details are called “trifles” by men, but they finally reveal the truth of Minnie’s life.

2. Artistic Techniques｜艺术技巧

The play uses contrast, symbolism, and dramatic irony. The men see the house only as a crime scene. They search in a professional and methodical way, but they ignore the kitchen and women’s daily experience. By contrast, the women see the house as a home. Through the broken jars, the unfinished sewing, the birdcage, and the dead bird, they gradually understand Minnie’s lonely and oppressive marriage. The bird symbolizes Minnie herself: once lively and singing, but finally silenced. The cage symbolizes marriage as confinement. The title is ironic because the “trifles” that men look down upon are actually the key to the case.

3. Significance｜影响 / 意义

The ending is especially important. Mrs. Hale and Mrs. Peters choose to hide the evidence. Their silence is no longer weakness. It becomes a conscious choice and a sign of female solidarity. In this way, the play questions a male-centered legal system and suggests another kind of justice based on sympathy, shared experience, and care.

The Egg｜Sherwood Anderson

Exam Use｜考法

Analytical Essay highest priority. It can also support questions on plot, characterization, symbolism, irony, point of view, and the American Dream.

Main Theme｜主旨

The Egg is an apparently uneventful story, but it becomes a parable of human defeat and a critique of the American passion for “getting up in the world.” It questions the pressure and illusion behind the American Dream.

Plot / Content｜内容

The plot is built around three failed ventures with eggs:

  1. The chicken farm: the family’s first attempt to rise in the world by nurturing eggs.
  2. The restaurant: the father tries to serve eggs instead of nurturing them, but few people come to eat.
  3. The egg performance: the father desperately tries to entertain Joe Kane with eggs, but the performance ends in failure and humiliation.

Techniques｜艺术技巧

  • Characterization: The father changes from an ordinary and possibly cheerful man into an ambitious, anxious, desperate, and finally defeated figure. His character is revealed through action, speech, gestures, emotion, and the narrator’s comments.
  • Symbolism: The egg symbolizes hope, the American Dream, fragility, failure, fate, and the futility of life.
  • Irony: The more the father tries to control the egg and achieve success, the more powerless and ridiculous he becomes.
  • Point of View: The story is mainly told by the son as a first-person non-protagonist narrator, creating distance, reflection, and a quiet ironic tone.
  • Style: The style is colloquial, simple, and marked by wry humor.

Form / Structure｜形式结构

The story has an apparently uneventful plot, but it is organized around repeated failures with eggs. The climax occurs when the father desperately tries to entertain Joe Kane. The repeated cycle of hope and failure strengthens the theme of futility.

  • The father’s character evolves because he is shaped and pressured by the American Dream.

Trifles｜Susan Glaspell

Exam Use｜考法

Analytical Essay highest priority. It can also support questions on feminist drama, gender relationships, symbolism, setting, dramatic irony, and women’s solidarity.

Main Theme｜主旨

Trifles is a murder mystery, but more importantly, it is an early feminist drama about gender relationships, power struggles, women’s oppression, and the nature of truth. It shows that what men dismiss as “trifles” actually reveals the truth of Minnie Wright’s life.

Plot / Content｜内容

John Wright has been murdered, and his wife Minnie Wright is suspected. The men search the house for legal evidence, while Mrs. Hale and Mrs. Peters discover the truth through small domestic details. In the end, the women choose to keep silent and hide the evidence.

Techniques｜艺术技巧

  • Contrast of Perspectives: Men see the house as a crime scene and search for legal clues; women see it as a home and reconstruct Minnie’s life through domestic details.
  • Setting / Spectacle: The cold, gloomy, untidy kitchen reflects Minnie’s lonely and loveless marriage.
  • Symbolism: The dead bird symbolizes Minnie’s silenced voice and lost freedom; the cage symbolizes marriage as confinement; the broken jars suggest a broken heart; the died-out fire suggests dead love or hope; the knot suggests both the murder weapon and the bond of female solidarity.
  • Dramatic Irony: Men laugh at women’s concerns as “trifles,” but these trifles lead to the real truth.
  • Characterization: Mrs. Hale and Mrs. Peters gradually move from ordinary observers to sympathetic women who understand Minnie’s pain and form solidarity with her.

Form / Structure｜形式结构

As a one-act play, Trifles is compact and concentrated. The simple murder investigation gradually reveals a deeper conflict between two ways of seeing: legal male judgment and female domestic understanding.

Key Sentences｜可背句

  • The play shows that women’s everyday experiences, though ignored by men, contain a truth that the official legal system fails to see.
  • The title is ironic because the so-called “trifles” are actually the key evidence.
  • The women’s final silence is not powerlessness, but a conscious choice and a mark of solidarity.
  • Trifles constructs an alternative idea of justice based on care, shared experience, and responsibility within relationships.
Analytical Essay Mini Version

Trifles is a murder mystery and an early feminist drama. It explores gender relationships, power struggles, and women’s oppression through a seemingly simple investigation. The men treat the kitchen and women’s concerns as unimportant, while Mrs. Hale and Mrs. Peters discover the truth through domestic “trifles.” Artistically, the play uses contrast, setting, symbolism, and dramatic irony. The cold kitchen, the dead bird, the cage, the broken jars, and the knot all reveal Minnie’s lonely marriage and emotional suffering. The play’s significance lies in its feminist challenge to patriarchal judgment. The women’s final silence becomes an act of choice and solidarity, showing that moral truth may exist outside official legal rules.

London presents urban misery, social oppression, institutional corruption, psychological imprisonment, and the loss of innocence.

Plot / Content｜内容

The speaker walks through London and observes suffering in every face and every voice. The poem moves from visual images in the first stanza to auditory images in the following stanzas.

  • Metaphor: “mind-forg’d manacles” suggests psychological oppression.
  • Oxymoron: “Marriage hearse” combines marriage and death, showing that love and family life are corrupted by disease, poverty, and social decay.

Form / Structure｜形式结构

The poem consists of four quatrains with an ABAB rhyme scheme. It is mainly written in iambic tetrameter, but metrical variations interrupt the walking rhythm and emphasize suffering.

  • The poem uses dark imagery and oppressive diction to criticize social, political, and religious institutions.

Through imagery, diction, repetition, metaphor, and oxymoron, Blake criticizes the oppressive structures of London and shows the loss of freedom and innocence.

The poem has four quatrains. It does not follow a strict rhyme scheme, but uses half rhyme and strong rhythmic movement.

Main Theme｜主旨

The poem explores moral hypocrisy and self-deception. The speaker condemns Brother Lawrence, but his speech actually reveals his own jealousy, hatred, and corruption.

Plot / Content｜内容

A jealous monk secretly curses Brother Lawrence and imagines ways to make him spiritually fall. In trying to condemn another monk, he exposes his own immoral mind.

Techniques｜艺术技巧

  • Unreliable Speaker: The speaker’s words cannot be trusted; he reveals more about himself than he intends.
  • Dramatic Irony: Readers see the speaker’s hypocrisy even though he presents himself as righteous.
  • Colloquial Language: Nonverbal sounds like “Gr-r-r” and curses create a vivid speaking voice.
  • Character Revelation: The speaker’s temperament and character are revealed through his own speech.
  • Contrast: Brother Lawrence appears morally better through the speaker’s hostile accusations.

Form / Structure｜形式结构

Nine eight-line stanzas. Rhyme scheme ABABCDCD. The meter is roughly trochaic tetrameter with irregularities. The language follows spoken expression.

Key Sentences｜可背句

  • The poem uses an unreliable speaker to expose hypocrisy.
  • The speaker’s hatred of Brother Lawrence reveals his own moral corruption.
  • Browning uses dramatic speech to let the speaker unintentionally give himself away.
