Poetry｜诗歌

课件框架：major types: narrative, lyric, dramatic；major elements: voice (speaker and tone), diction, imagery, figures of speech, sound and rhythm。重点为 prosody 与 imagery。

1. Poetry 与 Prosody

Poetry：composition in verse or metrical language, or an equivalent patterned arrangement of language；通常包含更精心选择的词语、比喻用法和一定的形式秩序。

3. Stanza / Rhyme Scheme / Verse Form

Stanza names

2 couplet；3 tercet/triplet；4 quatrain；5 quintain/cinquain；6 sextain；7 septet；8 octave；9 Spenserian stanza；14 sonnet。

High-frequency schemes

Heroic couplet: aa bb cc…；Ballad: abcb / abab；Terza rima: aba bcb cdc；Spenserian stanza: ababbcbcc。

Sonnets

Shakespearean: abab cdcd efef gg；Petrarchan: octave + sestet，课件示例 abbaabba cdccdc；Spenserian: abab bcbc cdcd ee。

4. Imagery, Diction, Voice

Imagery 意象

Representation through language of sense experience: sight, sound, smell, taste, touch, internal sensation, motion.

识别：不是“提供信息”，而是“show experience”；具体、可感、能引起联想。

Diction 措辞

关注 sound、denotation、connotation。诗歌语言倾向 short and concise、concrete、suggestive、multidimensional，并追求 novelty / defamiliarization。

Speaker 说话者

poet ≠ speaker。Reliable speaker 的态度大体代表诗人；unreliable speaker 的态度与诗人不同，戏剧独白中常见。

Tone 语气

作品态度的 emotional coloring；由 meter/rhyme、word choice、syntax、figures、imagery 共同产生。

Novel｜小说

Novel：an extended prose narrative about largely fictional, if usually realistic, characters and plausible events.

1. Rise and development

  • 18th century：一般被视为 English novel 的诞生期。
  • 条件：Enlightenment 对 reason / actuality 的重视；印刷技术与市场发展；城市人口识字率提高。
  • 早期代表：Defoe, Robinson Crusoe (1719), Moll Flanders (1722)；Swift, Gulliver’s Travels (1726)。
  • 复习内容 PDF 另列：欧洲第一部小说 Don Quixote (1605)；英国第一部小说 Robinson Crusoe (1719)。

4. Characterization

简答题重点：Two ways of characterization = direct characterization + indirect characterization。
  • Direct characterization：the narrator/author directly tells or comments on a character’s appearance or personality；常以外貌、名称、直接说明呈现。
  • Indirect characterization：the author steps aside and lets characters reveal themselves through dialogue, action, behavior, feeling and others’ reactions；reader infers the trait。

5. Style

Style = the verbal identity of the work，即 how to write。观察 diction、syntax、figures of speech、irony、symbol 与 narrative tone。

Drama｜戏剧

Drama 同时是 literary art 与 performing art；西方戏剧传统源于 ancient Greece，Greek tragedy 源于向 Dionysus 致敬的 dithyramb。

1. Aristotle’s Six Elements

Plot→Character→Thought→Diction→Spectacle→Music

Plot

“the soul of tragedy”；情节结构、冲突与因果。

Thought

the reasoning aspect；概括 moral 并指向全剧 symbolic meaning。

Diction

dynamic、clear、oral、interesting but economical、appropriate to character and situation。

Spectacle / Music

所有视觉方面 / 所有听觉方面；分别营造环境、气氛与 mood。

2. Plot Structure

Exposition→Point of attack / conflict→Rising action & complication→Climax / reversal→Falling action→Denouement

Feminist Criticism｜女性主义批评

Definition：examines how literature and other cultural productions reinforce or undermine the oppression of women.

1. Three Waves

First wave (1848–1920)

abolitionism and women’s suffrage；1920–1960 有 setback。

Second wave (mid-1960s–1982)

pill、abortion、equal pay；揭示 sexual harassment、domestic violence。

Third wave (1982–课件所称“now”)

关注 black women、lesbians，以及 race / sexual orientation。

2. Core Terms

术语中文课件定义作品联结
Patriarchy父权制a system/culture privileging men over women by promoting traditional gender rolesTrifles 中男性贬低女性经验。
Gender roles性别角色patriarchy prescribes men as rational/strong/decisive and women as emotional/weak/submissiveTrifles；The Egg 中男性经济成功压力。
Biological essentialism生物本质主义bases social inequality on biological differences and assumes women’s inborn inferiority为男性垄断社会权力辩护。
Social constructionism社会建构主义feminine and masculine gender categories are constructed by society“One is not born a woman; one becomes one.”
The Other他者woman is objectified and marginalized by difference from male norms女性被定义为“缺少男性所拥有之物”。
Sisterhood姐妹情谊psychological and political bonding based on common experiences and goalsTrifles 结尾的女性团结与沉默。

3. Premises & Practices

  • Women are oppressed economically, politically, socially and psychologically by patriarchy and patriarchal ideology.
  • Western institutions and literary canon are deeply rooted in male-oriented thinking.
  • Biology determines sex；culture determines gender；gender concerns socially programmed behavior, not anatomy.
  • Ultimate goal：promote women’s equality and change the world.
  • Practices：gender studies；sex-related writing strategies；Marxist feminism；psychoanalytic feminism；minority feminist criticism。

Comparison Questions｜辨析题

答题方法：先指出比较维度，再分别给定义，最后用一句“核心差异”收束。不要只写中文标签。
Sex vs Gender 连续真题 / 高频
SexGender
中文生理性别社会性别
Definitionbiological constitution as female or male, determined by naturecultural programming as feminine or masculine, created by society
Focusanatomy / biological traitssocially programmed behavior, roles and expectations
One-line distinctionBiology determines sex, whereas culture determines gender.
Story vs Plot 因果 / 时间

Story：a narrative of events arranged in their time sequence.

Plot：a narrative of events with emphasis on causality；time sequence remains, but causality overshadows it.

Story: “The king died and then the queen died.”
Plot: “The king died and then the queen died of grief.”

Round vs Flat Character

Round：well-developed, complex, three-dimensional, rich and convincing；may contain contradictory qualities.

Flat：underdeveloped, one-dimensional or stereotypical；often describable by one or two traits.

核心维度：complexity / degree of development。

Static vs Dynamic Character

Static：does not undergo significant change.

Dynamic：undergoes significant change or development.

易错：round/flat 主要看复杂度，static/dynamic 主要看是否变化。材料中常把 round 与 dynamic、flat 与 static 配对描述，但答辨析题时应分清两个判断维度。
Direct vs Indirect Characterization 连续真题

Direct：the narrator/author directly states or comments on appearance and personality — telling.

Indirect：the author lets speech, action, behavior, feeling and others’ reactions reveal personality — showing and inference.

Protagonist / Antagonist / Foil

Protagonist：main character involved in the conflict driving the plot；不必然是 moral hero。

Antagonist：a character or force in conflict with the protagonist；可为 nature / society / fate。

Foil：through difference or similarity, brings out an aspect of another character。

Blank Verse vs Free Verse

Blank verse：unrhymed iambic pentameter — meter without rhyme.

Free verse：without regular metrical patterns；may be rhymed or unrhymed.

Alliteration / Assonance / Consonance

Alliteration：initial consonant repetition；Assonance：vowel sound repetition；Consonance：consonant sound repetition, especially non-initial/end patterns in the course materials.

Modernism / Modernity / Modernization 期末重点标注可能考
概念材料中的定位核心说明
Modernismart / 文艺观念19世纪后半叶出现、20世纪之交兴盛；质疑和批判理性主义，打破传统艺术形式，探索新的现实呈现方式；材料举 Baudelaire 与 Eliot。
Modernityideology / 性质源于 Enlightenment 的思想架构；追求人的主体性与自主性，是西方资本主义社会的理论根基。
Modernizationinstitution / 制度与过程思想在城市、工业、商业、教育、经济与社会运作方式中的制度化、成型化。

Modernism concerns artistic response; modernity concerns the ideological condition; modernization concerns institutional and social transformation.

Tragedy vs Comedy

Tragedy：concentrates on man’s destiny and philosophical man；通过 pity/fear 与 katharsis 显示人面对毁灭性力量时的尊严。

Comedy：lives in commonplace everyday life and focuses on social man；揭示 follies, foibles and vices，通常以肯定性和快乐结局收束。

Dramatic Monologue vs Soliloquy

Dramatic monologue：single persona addresses an implied/silent auditor；auditor 通过说话线索被感知。

Soliloquy：speaker talks to himself without an explicit listener；Soliloquy of the Spanish Cloister 是 self-addressed interior commentary，但共享 dramatic monologue 的自我暴露与心理刻画特征。

Key Works｜重点作品

William Blake, “London” (1794)

赏析题重点题库高频
Genre
lyric poem；四个 quatrains。
Theme
urban suffering and misery；oppression and control；loss of innocence；institutional corruption；psychological oppression。
Form / Structure
four stanzas × four lines；ABAB throughout；主要为 iambic tetrameter，但存在 metric variation。第一节偏视觉，后三节更聚焦声音；第三节行首形成 “HEAR” 的 acrostic（评述资料强调）。
Diction
“charter’d” 暗示 mapped, regulated, controlled；“every”反复扩大苦难的普遍性；大量负面词汇制造 inescapable misery。
Imagery
marks of weakness/woe；cries；chimney-sweeper；black’ning Church；soldier’s blood；harlot’s curse；new-born infant；Marriage hearse。
Figures
repetition；metaphor (“mind-forg’d manacles”)；oxymoron (“Marriage hearse”)；enjambment；caesura；alliteration；symbolism。
Speaker / Tone
a disillusioned observer walking through London；gloomy, critical, indignant, despairing。
Exam angles
“charter’d”的含义；sight → sound；institutional targets；imagery 如何共同表现 oppression；regular form 与 chaotic misery 的张力。
英文答题句：

Through the repeated word “every,” Blake presents suffering as universal rather than accidental.

The metaphor “mind-forg’d manacles” suggests that oppression has been internalized as a psychological form of imprisonment.

The regular ABAB rhyme scheme contrasts with the misery described, making urban suffering appear repetitive and inescapable.

William Shakespeare, “Sonnet 130”

赏析题重点题库高频
Genre
Shakespearean sonnet。
Theme
realistic and authentic love；critique of artificial, hyperbolic praise；love values reality rather than superficial ideals。
Form / Structure
three quatrains + final rhyming couplet；ABAB CDCD EFEF GG；iambic pentameter。前三组比较拆解理想化惯例，末尾 couplet 完成意义转折与确认。
Diction
simple, direct, deliberately unornamented；多用 negation (“nothing like,” “no such,” “never”)。
Imagery
visual: sun, coral, snow, black wires, roses；olfactory: perfumes/breath；aural: speech/music；kinesthetic: goddess walking / mistress treading on ground。
Figures
irony, parody, anti-hyperbole / subverted comparisons, negation, contrast。
Speaker / Tone
realistic, witty, ironic toward false poetic convention, yet affectionate toward the mistress。
Exam angles
anti-Petrarchan strategy；final couplet；real beauty vs false compare；不同感官意象；形式传统与内容反传统的结合。
英文答题句：

The speaker rejects conventional comparisons not to insult his mistress, but to expose the artificiality of exaggerated love poetry.

The final couplet reveals that sincere love does not depend on false comparison or idealized physical beauty.

The formal regularity of the Shakespearean sonnet frames a deliberately unconventional and realistic praise of love.

Emily Dickinson, “I like to see it lap the Miles”

赏析题重点
Genre
riddle-like lyric describing a train without naming it。
Theme
industrialization, technological power, human ingenuity；nature and machine；admiration mixed with awareness of force and intrusion。
Form / Structure
课件正文为17行并大量使用 dash；复习资料对具体 stanza 数有冲突，考试更稳妥地抓 irregular/half-rhyme、enjambment、动作推进与最后 stop。
Diction
dynamic verbs: lap, lick, feed, step, peer, pare, crawl, chase, neigh, stop；“prodigious,” “supercilious,” “horrid,” “docile and omnipotent”塑造兼具强大与驯服的形象。
Imagery
train as a large animal/horse；valleys, mountains, shanties, quarry, hill, stable door；aural imagery in “complaining,” “hooting,” “neigh”。
Figures
extended personification / animal metaphor；simile (“neigh like Boanerges”)；anaphora (“And” / “Then”)；alliteration；enjambment；capitalization and dashes。
Speaker / Tone
observing voice with wonder, fascination and respect for industrial power；diction also carries hints of arrogance and menace。
Exam angles
如何从 verbs 判断“it”是 train；personification；sound imagery；“docile and omnipotent”并置；运动节奏与工业力量。
英文答题句：

Dickinson never names the train; instead, a chain of animated verbs turns the machine into a powerful living creature.

The phrase “docile and omnipotent” captures the paradoxical image of a machine that is both controllable and immensely powerful.

The repeated conjunctions and enjambment reproduce the train’s continuous movement through the landscape.

Robert Browning, “Soliloquy of the Spanish Cloister”

赏析题重点Speaker / Tone 高频
Genre
dramatic soliloquy；与 dramatic monologue 共享人物自我暴露与心理刻画。
Theme
moral hypocrisy, jealousy, hatred, religious corruption / immorality；speaker 把自己的罪恶投射给 Brother Lawrence。
Form / Structure
nine eight-line stanzas；ABABCDCD；roughly trochaic tetrameter with irregularities。
Diction
spoken language；nonverbal growl “Gr-r-r”；colloquial curses；Latin religious expressions；coarse, violent, sarcastic vocabulary。
Imagery
flowers, food, tableware, convent figures, damnation, Satan；日常细节被 speaker 的仇恨扭曲。
Figures
dramatic irony；self-revelation；allusion；alliteration；caesura；contrast / juxtaposition。
Speaker / Tone
a jealous, hateful, hypocritical monk speaking to himself；sarcastic, contemptuous, bitter, malevolent。
Exam angles
who speaks / tone / listener / comparison with Brother Lawrence；unreliable speaker；表面虔诚与内在罪恶；规则形式与混乱心理。
英文答题句：

The speaker condemns Brother Lawrence, yet his violent language exposes his own jealousy and moral corruption.

Dramatic irony arises because the monk’s accusations reveal more about himself than about the man he hates.

The regular stanza and rhyme pattern sharply contrast with the speaker’s uncontrolled and sinful thoughts.

Susan Glaspell, Trifles (1916)

文本赏析重点女性主义联结
Genre
one-act play；murder mystery；early feminist drama。
Theme
gender relationships and power struggle；women’s oppression；male blindness；female awakening and solidarity；law versus empathetic justice。
Form / Structure
crime investigation with exposition / complication / denouement；men’s official investigation and women’s informal reconstruction form two contrasting modes。
Setting
a bleak, untidy, cold kitchen in an abandoned rural farmhouse；domestic space becomes the real site of knowledge。
Diction / Dialogue
men’s dismissive and patronizing language contrasts with women’s reflective, collaborative dialogue；speech reveals gender power。
Symbols
cold kitchen = loveless marriage；died-out fire = lost love/hope；broken jars = broken heart；bird = Minnie/women；cage = marriage；knot = sewing, rope, marriage and female solidarity。
Perspective
Men see a crime scene and follow rules/facts；women see a home, remember, empathize, imagine and reconstruct Minnie’s life。
Exam angles
title irony；dead bird as motive；two investigative methods；symbols；women’s final silence as intentional solidarity；feminist criticism。

英文答题句：

The men dismiss domestic details as “trifles,” but these neglected details enable the women to reconstruct Minnie’s oppressed life and discover the motive.

The kitchen functions not merely as a setting but as a record of emotional isolation and patriarchal control.

By concealing the dead bird, Mrs. Hale and Mrs. Peters replace the rigid logic of law with an ethic of care and female solidarity.

Sherwood Anderson, “The Egg”

文本赏析重点symbol / POV 高频
Genre
short fiction / short story。
Theme
the American passion for getting up in the world；ambition, failure and disillusionment；fragility of human aspiration；futility and fate；pressure of patriarchal success。
Plot / Structure
uneventful, character-driven；three unsuccessful egg ventures：chicken farm → restaurant → entertaining a customer with eggs。Climax：father desperately tries to entertain Joe Kane。
Character
father = round, active protagonist；mother = flat and static；antagonist = fate / American passion for upward success。
Characterization
father through appearance, action, speech, feeling and others’ comments；mother mainly through appearance and role；both direct and indirect methods。
Style
colloquial, with wry humor；irony and detailed gestures build the climax。
Point of View
first-person narrator as non-protagonist (the son)；课件指出后段转向 third-person omniscient narration。
Symbols
egg = hope / American Dream；fragility / broken idealism；uncooperative fate；human beings in fate’s hands；egg-hen cycle = cycle of fate。Grotesque chicken mirrors father’s transformation into a human grotesque。
Exam angles
egg 的多重象征；father 的 character arc；narrator choice；wry humor / irony；American Dream 与失败。
英文答题句：

The egg symbolizes both hope and fragility: it promises new life and success, yet its thin shell anticipates failure and broken idealism.

The father’s increasingly desperate performance turns him into a grotesque shaped by his naïve faith in upward success.

The son’s retrospective narration connects the father’s failure with the lasting psychological cost imposed on the next generation.

William Shakespeare, A Midsummer Night’s Dream

2. Characterization

There are two major ways of characterization: direct and indirect characterization. Direct characterization tells the reader what a character is like through explicit description or comment. Indirect characterization allows the character’s personality to emerge through speech, action, behavior, feeling, and other characters’ reactions.

诗歌五段式

  1. Introduction / thesis：作者、题目、体裁、核心主题，以及形式如何服务主题。
  2. Form and structure：stanza、rhyme、meter、enjambment / caesura、结构转折。
  3. Diction and imagery：具体词语与感官意象，不要只列名称。
  4. Figures, speaker and tone：修辞如何暴露态度或塑造声音。
  5. Conclusion：回扣 thesis，概括形式与意义的统一。

小说 / 戏剧五段式

  1. Introduction：genre、setting / narrative situation、central conflict、theme。
  2. Plot and perspective：情节推进、climax、POV 或两种调查模式。

  5. Conclusion：形式选择如何深化社会/性别/理想主题。

冲突点材料差异本体系处理
“London” meter单选与课件扫描支持 iambic tetrameter；判断题某条解析误写 iambic pentameter。采用 iambic tetrameter + variations。
“Spanish Cloister” meterPoetry PPT slide 88 与多数复习稿写 roughly trochaic tetrameter；文学评述3误写 iambic tetrameter。采用课件：trochaic tetrameter with irregularities。
Dickinson stanza count评述资料出现“4 quatrains + quintain”与“6 quatrains”等互相冲突说法；PPT正文显示17行但未明确分节标签。不把有争议的 stanza count 设为死记结论；优先掌握17行文本、dashes、personification 与 movement。
Round/flat 与 dynamic/static课件常用配对式简化；常识资料又分别给出“复杂度”和“变化”定义。辨析时按两个维度作答，同时记住 The Egg 对人物的课件分类。
